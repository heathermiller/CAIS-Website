Workshops & Tutorials

Join us on Tuesday, May 26, 2026—the day before the main conference—for focused sessions on compound AI systems research and practice.

Call for Proposals

Researchers and practitioners are invited to submit proposals for workshops and tutorials in conjunction with the 1st ACM Conference on AI and Agentic Systems (CAIS 2026).

The workshops and tutorials aim to provide a forum for exchanging opinions, presenting ideas, and discussing preliminary results with a diverse group of conference attendees.

Topics of Interest

We welcome proposals covering topics including, but not limited to:

System Design

Design and optimization of compound AI systems, multi-agent architectures, and inference-time scaling strategies.

Infrastructure

Systems and infrastructure for building, deploying, and operating compound AI systems at scale.

Frameworks

Tutorials on frameworks like DSPy, LangChain, and other tools for building compound AI systems.

Proposal Requirements

Submission Format

Proposals should be no more than 2 pages, plus references. The 2 pages should encompass the workshop/tutorial description and information about the organizers.

Submissions must follow the ACM SIGPLAN template:

All Proposals Must Include

  • Workshop/tutorial name and a 150–200 word abstract suitable for the conference website
  • Summary of topic, scope, and significance, including relevance to CAIS themes
  • Information about past iterations (if any)
  • Whether the event is half-day or full-day
  • Efforts to include diverse participants (mentoring, subsidies, inclusive topic framing)
  • Brief CV/bio of each organizer with contact info and a single primary contact

For Workshops

  • Paper/presentation selection procedure
  • Expected number of participants and planned format
  • Tentative schedule (submission, notification, camera-ready dates)
  • Program committee members already chosen

For Tutorials

  • Detailed schedule and planned activities
  • What the tutorial will cover and what participants need to follow along

Selection Criteria

All proposals will be judged on their merits using the following criteria. Note that workshops/tutorials organized at previous conferences (NeurIPS, ICLR, SOSP, ICML, etc.) will not be automatically accepted.

Topic Importance

Is the workshop focused on a clear, topical problem the community will find interesting and useful?

Discussion & Community

Does the program offer opportunities for discussion and community building?

Diversity & Inclusion

Commitment to diversity and inclusion in all forms.

Organizer Experience

Organizational experience, potential, and ability of the team.
