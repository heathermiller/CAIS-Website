Call for Papers

ACM Conference on Agentic and AI Systems

San Francisco • May 26–29, 2026

Overview

CAIS 2026 invites original research contributions on agentic and compound AI systems—architectures that advance the state of the art through principled composition, intelligent scaling, and systematic verification.

We welcome submissions that push boundaries in:

  • Principled composition of multiple system components
  • Smart inference-time scaling strategies
  • Systematic verification methods for reliable deployment
  • Novel evaluation frameworks for compound systems

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions across four key pillars:

Architectural Patterns & Composition

  • Networks of Networks and inference-time scaling
  • Verifier-based systems
  • RAG architectures
  • Multi-agent coordination
  • Tool-augmented AI
  • Modular system design

System Optimization & Efficiency

  • End-to-end pipeline optimization
  • Cost-performance trade-offs
  • Resource allocation strategies
  • Automated architecture search
  • Latency and throughput optimization
  • Model routing strategies

Engineering & Operations

  • MLOps for compound AI
  • Monitoring and observability
  • Specification languages
  • Security and safety
  • Testing methodologies

Evaluation & Benchmarking

  • Performance metrics

  • Real-world impact assessment
  • Benchmark design
  • Human evaluation studies

Submission Tracks

Research Papers

Original research contributions presenting novel architectures, algorithms, systems, or empirical studies.

  • Format: Double-blind review
  • Page limit: (to be announced)

System Demonstrations

Working implementations with technical description.

  • Format: Short paper (4 pages) + demonstration artifact
  • Review: Evaluated on technical contribution and demonstration quality
Workshops & Tutorials: Separate calls will be announced for hands-on sessions on tools, frameworks, and methodologies.

Review Process & Reproducibility

Double-Blind Peer Review

All research papers undergo rigorous double-blind peer review. Authors must:

  • Anonymize submissions (remove author names, affiliations)
  • Avoid self-identifying citations
  • Declare conflicts of interest

Artifact Evaluation

CAIS encourages artifact submission to support reproducibility. We adopt ACM's artifact badging taxonomy:

  • Artifacts Available: Materials publicly available
  • Artifacts Evaluated - Functional: Documented, consistent, complete, exercisable
  • Results Reproduced: Main results independently obtained

Publication Ethics

All submissions must follow ACM Publication Policies:

  • No concurrent submissions
  • No plagiarism or self-plagiarism
  • Proper attribution
  • Conflict disclosure
